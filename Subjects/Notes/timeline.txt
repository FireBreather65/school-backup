~ 7 year gap ~
1763: The Seven Years' War (aka French and Indian War) ends due to the Treaty of Paris, Royal Proclamation of 1763 (a British-imposed boundary line to end white settlement west of the Appalachians, although ignored by the American colonists)
1764: Sugar Act enacted (to stop the smuggling of molasses into the American colonies from the West Indies by cutting taxes on it, designed to increase British revenue to pay back their debt)
1765: Stamp Act enacted (imposing a direct tax on all printed material in North American colonies, raising money to pay back the British Empire's debt), colonial unrest ensues
1766: Sugar and Stamp Act repealed (due to revolt from American colonists)
1767-68: Townshend Acts passed (again to pay back British Empire's debt)
~ 2 year gap ~
1770: Boston Massacre (opened fire and killed 5 townspeople, due to an angry mob harassing the soldiers)
~ 3 year gap ~
1773: The Boston Tea Party occurs (as a direct response to British taxation policies in North America)
1774: Coercive Acts passed by British Parliament (nicknamed by American colonists as the "Intolerable Acts")
^ the Coercive (Intolerable) Acts consisted of:
	- the Boston Port Bill (fining Boston for the tea destroyed in 1773, during the BTP)
	- the Government Bill (rewriting Massachusetts' charter granting broadly expanded powers to the royal governor)
	- the Administration of Justice Act (authorizing the governor to send indicted government officials to other colonies or even London for trial)
	- the Quartering Act (allowing British troops to be housed in private buildings, setting them as a priority to even homeless folks)
1775: The Battles of Lexington and Concord (the first military clashes of the American Revolutionary War), Revolutionary War begins due to colonial unrest coming to a climax
1776: The Declaration of Independence is issued (America gained independence from Britain)
1777: Congress adopts Articles of Confederation
~ 4 year gap ~
