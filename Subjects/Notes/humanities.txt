Source Identification:

  - photography
	- a diary
	- a speech
	- even a shopping list (depending on what you're researching)

- secondary source is your interpretation of what happened

Analyzing a Historical Text:
- find out what the people are vs. aren't talking about
^ using the photography analogy, what's outside their view? What's inside their view?

- also find out what the source can tell you (and what it doesn't tell you)

Avoiding Common Mistakes in Historical Essays:
- avoid the following sayings:
  - "Throughout history..."
	- "It was inevitable..."
	- "And that's why [insert country] is so great today!"

- instead use:
  - "In the [insert specific time period] in the [insert place(s)]"
	- "The [insert catalyst] caused [insert effect of catalyst]"

2nd Lowest: Mestizos (children of a Spaniard + Native American) and Mulatoes (children of a Spaniard + African slave)
Lowest: Indios (children of two Native American parents) and Negroes (children of two African parents)

The Market Revolution:
- often debated whether [...] or the American Revolution was more revolutionary

- included many revolutions, such as:
	- Industrial Revolution
	- Transportation/Communication Revolution
	- broader Market Revolution

- regarding the Industrial Revolution:
	- new inventions that had come to the US completely revolutionized how things were made (such as the textile mill, the water wheel, the sewing machine, and cotton gin, amongst other things)

	- the US went from being a nation of farmers to being a nation of people who worked for wages per hour (and bought things with those wages)

	- in the beginning, mainly women worked in factories because young men had a good path forward in life (like farming, learning a trade), whereas women didn't really have a place outside of the home, also because they were associated with the fabric/sewing and you could pay them less for the same amount of work (the origin of the gender pay gap???)

	- to add to the above, women dealt with brutal conditions (no air-conditioning, ridiculously low wages, and 12-hour days) partly because again, they had no other opportunities outside of the house, but also because they were able to finally make money by themselves

	- cotton gin (invented by Eli Whitney) revolutionized how much cotton one person could make in a single day (about 50 pounds) <- this also bolstered the institution of slavery in the South, the exact opposite of what we want...


- regarding the Transportation/Communication Revolution:
	- new inventions during the early 19th century also allowed goods and information to be transported faster (such as the railroad, the steamship, canals, and the telegraph, amongst other things)

	- as a result of the above, the scope and pace of business grew (canals and the telegraph affected each consecutively, again, amongst other things)


- regarding the broader Market Revolution:
	- people stopped being their own bosses, instead they report to other bosses

	- the rise of the assembly-line system leads to fewer and fewer artisans making the whole product from start to finish
	^ people also stop taking pride in their own work

	- the US also develops a market economy, meaning people made private deals with investors all over the US and the world

	- after the war of 1812, the US went through about a 20 year period of an unstable boom/bust economy (which included an economic depression in 1819, also called the Panic of 1819)

The Mexican-American War:
- the two major causes of [...] were Texas annexation and "Manifest Destiny"

- Texas annexation was the concept of American slave owners wanting to expand, so they moved to Texas (which was still controlled by Spain)

- after Mexico gained independence, they outlawed slavery (to the resistence of the above settlers)

- after Texas gained independence from Mexico, they requested annexation, but the US believed adding another slave state to the Union was politically dangerous

- until 1845, Texas was an independent nation - what changed is that ardent expansionist (and believer in Manifest Destiny) James K. Polk took office and annexed Texas as soon after taking office as possible

- he also wanted California, but Mexico wasn't selling - so he directed General Zachary Taylor (yes, a future president) to provoke hostilities in the disputed territory (between Mexico and Texas)

- the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo ended the war - the US gave $15M to Mexico, and Mexico ceded most of the current Southwest to the US

Sectional Conflict:
- economics:
	- North: originally focused on trade and manufacturing (too cold for plantations), moved on to factories once Market Revolution happened, had a growing middle class (the lower classes hoped to expand westward and start a family farm to grow financially stable)
	- South: focused on plantation agriculture (didn't industrialize because cash crops did so well), wealth measured by number of owned slaves, the lower classes hoped to expand westward to own their own plantation and expand cotton

- ideologies:
	- North: most whites didn't object to slavery in the South, but worried about the potential expansion of slavery to the West, "Slave Power" in the government, abolition of the moral evil of slavery
	- South: believed the Consitution protected expansion of slavery, thought that North was trying to take their freedom and rights, George Fitzhugh claimed that slavery was better than capitalism (and poor white people should be enslaved as well as African people)
