Source Identification:
- primary source is the main source, such as:
  - photography
	- a diary
	- a speech
	- even a shopping list (depending on what you're researching)

- secondary source is your interpretation of what happened

~

Analyzing a Historical Text:
- find out what the people are vs. aren't talking about
^ using the photography analogy, what's outside their view? What's inside their view?

- also find out what the source can tell you (and what it doesn't tell you)

~

Avoiding Common Mistakes in Historical Essays:
- avoid the following sayings:
  - "Throughout history..."
	- "It was inevitable..."
	- "And that's why [insert country] is so great today!"

- instead use:
  - "In the [insert specific time period] in the [insert place(s)]"
	- "The [insert catalyst] caused [insert effect of catalyst]"

Spanish Colonization:
- Encomiendas (a combination of feudalism and slavery)
- it granted land holders the right to labor of Native Americans (WHA??)
- anything they produced through their slave labor gave them Christianization and "protection" of Spanish feudal lords (aka encomenderos)

- the Native population shrunk to 10% of its former size, so they started using enslaved Africans for labor instead

- adopting Christianity outwardly, while keeping their ancestral beliefs inwardly

- the Pueblo Revolt included:
	- killing Spanish priests
	- burning churches (replacing them with kivas)
	- driving the Spanish out	

- racial caste system (ranked individuals by how much Spanish was in their blood, the higher amount, the more legal rights you have)"

Highest: Peninsulares (children of two Spaniards born in Spain)
2nd Highest: Criollos (children of two Spaniards born in America)
2nd Lowest: Mestizos (children of a Spaniard + Native American) and Mulatoes (children of a Spaniard + African slave)
Lowest: Indios (children of two Native American parents) and Negroes (children of two African parents)

Comparing European and Native [...] cultures:
- land:
  - Europeans: land indiviually owned, passed down in families

French and Dutch Colonization:
- much less violent than the Spanish Colonization, more friendly and observant/aware of each others' rituals
^ but it was self-servient, because they traded with each other (the fur trade)

comparing colonization:
- goal:
  - Spain: gold plantations
	- France + Netherlands: trading furs and fish
	- England: plantations + religious freedom
- settlers:
	- Spain: mostly men in large numbers
	- France + Netherlands: mostly men in small numbers
	- England: men in the South, families in the North
- native relations:
  - Spain: violence, slavery, intermarriage
	- France + Netherlands: friendly, intermarriage
	- England: violence, excursion
- religion:
  - Spain: forced conversion to Catholicism
	- France + Netherlands: attempted conversion to Catholicism
	- England: some attempted conversion to Protestantism

Jamestown - [...]:
- De La Warr (pronounced "delaware") comes in and changes the little-to-no conflict of the Powhatans and the English to an all-out war (because he wants to get rid of the Powhatans)

- he raids their villages, burns their crops, and basically erases any semblance of peace between the two nations

- they start cultivating tobacco (once John Rolfe discovers that Virginia is the perfect place to grow it)
^ but of course, they imported African slaves to cultivate it
^ there were also (mainly) indentured servants from England (who, after a few years, became the person they worked for aka a planter)

- so actually, regarding above, in the early 1600s, it was mainly white indentured servants (not until later on was it mainly African slaves), because of expenses (slaves were hundreds of pounds, whereas indentured servants were only a couple of pounds) and because said servants tended to die before they served their entire indenture, as swampy Virginia was not a great place to live due to mosquitoes and accompanying diseases
^ but if they did survive, the headright system ensured that they would get some land

- African slavery became the predominate form of labor around 1700 because of Bacon's Rebellion

- Bacon's Rebellion was a biracial (black and white men) raid force against the Native Americans (because they want their land) and against the House of Burgesses (Virginia government)

- [The above] is related to slavery because they figured that continuing the headright system would lead to constant rebellion, so they decided to find people who would never get freedom or land (they chose African slaves)

The Declaration of Independence:
- one of the reasons they signed the Declaration of Independence was that they wanted war help (esp. from France, a long time enemy of Great Britain)
^ it worked, and allowed them to win the Revolutionary War

- the Enlightenment was a period in the 1600s and 1700s, when people began to explore scientific observation + reason, instead of blindly accepting religious explanations

- the book "Two Treatises on Government" influenced many thinkers of the time period (during the Enlightenment), and had 2 main points - the idea of natural rights (rights endowed by nature that all people were born with including life, liberty, and property, in contrast to English thinking that there is a "divine right of kings"), and the idea of the Social Contract (the thought that government came from the people, a democracy)
^ he also suggested that a tyrannical government (one that doesn't abide by the Social Contract) can be rebelled against and overthrown
